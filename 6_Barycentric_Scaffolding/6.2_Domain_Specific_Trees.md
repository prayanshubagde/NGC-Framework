# Barycentric Scaffolding: Domain-Specific Examples

**Date:** January 18, 2026  
**Author:** Nathanael J. Bocker

## 1. Introduction

This document provides concrete examples of how to construct barycentric trees for five different domains: Agriculture, Healthcare, Finance, Energy, and Materials Science. Each example follows the methodology outlined in `6.1_Scaffolding_Construction_Methodology.md`.

## 2. The Root Simplex (Universal)

All domains share the same root simplex, grounding them in fundamental physics:

-   **Vertices:** (E, M, v, t) - Energy, Mass, Velocity, Time
-   **Type:** 4D Tetrahedron

## 3. Domain 1: Agriculture

### 3.1 Primary Simplex

-   **Vertices:** (WEATHER, SOIL, CROP)
-   **Type:** 3D Triangle
-   **Parent:** `ROOT` (or a `MATTER` sub-simplex)

### 3.2 Secondary Simplices

-   **WEATHER:** (TEMPERATURE, PRECIPITATION, WIND)
-   **SOIL:** (NUTRIENTS, MOISTURE, PH)
-   **CROP:** (GROWTH_STAGE, HEALTH, YIELD)

### 3.3 Example Concept Mapping

-   **Drought:**
    -   **Path:** `ROOT → (WEATHER, SOIL, CROP)`
    -   **Coordinates:** (0.8, 0.1, 0.1) - Primarily a `WEATHER` phenomenon

-   **Fertilizer:**
    -   **Path:** `ROOT → (WEATHER, SOIL, CROP) → (NUTRIENTS, MOISTURE, PH)`
    -   **Coordinates:** (0.9, 0.1, 0.0) - Primarily a `NUTRIENTS` concept

## 4. Domain 2: Healthcare

### 4.1 Primary Simplex

-   **Vertices:** (PATHOGEN, HOST, ENVIRONMENT)
-   **Type:** 3D Triangle
-   **Parent:** `ROOT`

### 4.2 Secondary Simplices

-   **PATHOGEN:** (VIRULENCE, TRANSMISSIBILITY, RESISTANCE)
-   **HOST:** (IMMUNITY, GENETICS, BEHAVIOR)
-   **ENVIRONMENT:** (DENSITY, SANITATION, CLIMATE)

### 4.3 Example Concept Mapping

-   **Epidemic:**
    -   **Path:** `ROOT → (PATHOGEN, HOST, ENVIRONMENT)`
    -   **Coordinates:** (0.4, 0.3, 0.3) - A balanced interaction between all three primes

-   **Vaccine:**
    -   **Path:** `ROOT → (PATHOGEN, HOST, ENVIRONMENT) → (IMMUNITY, GENETICS, BEHAVIOR)`
    -   **Coordinates:** (0.9, 0.1, 0.0) - Primarily an `IMMUNITY` concept

## 5. Domain 3: Finance

### 5.1 Primary Simplex

-   **Vertices:** (LIQUIDITY, VOLATILITY, SENTIMENT)
-   **Type:** 3D Triangle
-   **Parent:** `ROOT`

### 5.2 Secondary Simplices

-   **LIQUIDITY:** (BID_ASK_SPREAD, VOLUME, MARKET_DEPTH)
-   **VOLATILITY:** (HISTORICAL, IMPLIED, REALIZED)
-   **SENTIMENT:** (FEAR, GREED, UNCERTAINTY)

### 4.3 Example Concept Mapping

-   **Flash Crash:**
    -   **Path:** `ROOT → (LIQUIDITY, VOLATILITY, SENTIMENT)`
    -   **Coordinates:** (0.6, 0.3, 0.1) - Primarily a `LIQUIDITY` crisis, with high `VOLATILITY`

-   **FOMO (Fear of Missing Out):**
    -   **Path:** `ROOT → (LIQUIDITY, VOLATILITY, SENTIMENT) → (FEAR, GREED, UNCERTAINTY)`
    -   **Coordinates:** (0.1, 0.8, 0.1) - Primarily a `GREED` phenomenon

## 6. Domain 4: Energy

### 6.1 Primary Simplex

-   **Vertices:** (GENERATION, DEMAND, STORAGE)
-   **Type:** 3D Triangle
-   **Parent:** `ROOT`

### 6.2 Secondary Simplices

-   **GENERATION:** (RENEWABLE, FOSSIL, NUCLEAR)
-   **DEMAND:** (INDUSTRIAL, RESIDENTIAL, COMMERCIAL)
-   **STORAGE:** (BATTERY, PUMPED_HYDRO, THERMAL)

### 4.3 Example Concept Mapping

-   **Blackout:**
    -   **Path:** `ROOT → (GENERATION, DEMAND, STORAGE)`
    -   **Coordinates:** (0.4, 0.5, 0.1) - `DEMAND` exceeds `GENERATION`, with insufficient `STORAGE`

-   **Solar Panel:**
    -   **Path:** `ROOT → (GENERATION, DEMAND, STORAGE) → (RENEWABLE, FOSSIL, NUCLEAR)`
    -   **Coordinates:** (1.0, 0.0, 0.0) - Purely a `RENEWABLE` generation concept

## 7. Domain 5: Materials Science

### 7.1 Primary Simplex

-   **Vertices:** (STRUCTURE, PROPERTY, PROCESS, PERFORMANCE)
-   **Type:** 4D Tetrahedron
-   **Parent:** `ROOT`

### 7.2 Secondary Simplices

-   **STRUCTURE:** (CRYSTAL, AMORPHOUS, COMPOSITE)
-   **PROPERTY:** (MECHANICAL, THERMAL, ELECTRICAL)
-   **PROCESS:** (SYNTHESIS, FABRICATION, TREATMENT)
-   **PERFORMANCE:** (DURABILITY, EFFICIENCY, COST)

### 4.3 Example Concept Mapping

-   **Steel:**
    -   **Path:** `ROOT → (STRUCTURE, PROPERTY, PROCESS, PERFORMANCE)`
    -   **Coordinates:** (0.4, 0.4, 0.1, 0.1) - A material defined by its `STRUCTURE` and `PROPERTY`

-   **3D Printing:**
    -   **Path:** `ROOT → (STRUCTURE, PROPERTY, PROCESS, PERFORMANCE) → (SYNTHESIS, FABRICATION, TREATMENT)`
    -   **Coordinates:** (0.1, 0.8, 0.1) - Primarily a `FABRICATION` process

## 8. Conclusion

These examples demonstrate the universality of the barycentric scaffolding. By selecting appropriate domain primes, any field of knowledge can be mapped into a geometrically consistent, physically grounded semantic space. This is the foundation for true cross-domain artificial intelligence.
