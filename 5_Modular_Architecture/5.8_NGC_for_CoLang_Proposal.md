## 3. A Proposed Geometric Encoding for CoLang Glyphs

Here is a concrete proposal for how to map the elements of a CoLang glyph directly into the NGC framework. This provides a mathematically rigorous and computationally efficient representation.

### 3.1. The Foundational Space: Triaxial Coordinates

Every CoLang glyph is represented as a geometric object within NGC's triaxial polar coordinate system. This space is defined by three diagonally perpendicular planes, providing a richer, more stable alternative to the Cartesian (x, y, z) system.

### 3.2. Mapping the Glyph Components

Each component of a CoLang glyph is assigned a specific geometric role:

*   **The Dot (The Self):**
    *   **NGC Representation:** The **origin point** of the triaxial coordinate system. It is the immutable center from which all thought, represented by vectors, originates. It can also be modeled as the primary **tetrahedral computational primitive**, the fundamental unit of structure in NGC.

*   **Lines (Cognitive State, Direction, Emphasis):**
    *   **NGC Representation:** A set of **vectors** originating from the Dot. The properties of these vectors define the cognitive state:
        *   **Direction:** The vector's orientation in triaxial space represents the direction of thought.
        *   **Length:** The vector's magnitude (`r` in polar coordinates) represents the **emphasis** or energy of the thought.
        *   **Relationships:** The geometric relationships between multiple vectors (e.g., parallel, orthogonal, intersecting) define the complexity and structure of the thought (e.g., "equal or parallel," "about a complex topic").

*   **Color (Emotional Context):**
    *   **NGC Representation:** This is the most powerful mapping. In NGC, emotion is not an arbitrary label but a direct measure of the **rate of change of the system's state**. We map emotional context to **Curvature (Ω)**, one of the three Leibniz-Bocker diagnostics.
        *   **Low Curvature (Ω ≈ 0):** Represents neutral, calm, or stable emotional states. The thought-vectors are straight or gently curving.
        *   **High Curvature (Ω > threshold):** Represents intense emotional states like joy, anger, fear, or surprise. The thought-vectors are sharply turning, spiraling, or changing direction rapidly.
        *   This provides a continuous, quantitative measure of emotional intensity, far richer than discrete labels.

*   **Environmental Modifiers (Funneling, Expansion, Constraint):**
    *   **NGC Representation:** These are **geometric transformations** applied to the vector space.
        *   **Funneling:** A **projection** that collapses a set of vectors onto a single, more focused path. This geometrically represents a convergence of thought.
        *   **Expansion:** A **scaling operation**, often using φ (the golden ratio), which expands the possibility space of the thought vectors.
        *   **Constraint:** A set of **boundary conditions** defined within the geometric space, which the thought-vectors cannot cross. This represents logical, ethical, or physical limitations.

### 3.3. A Dynamic System

A CoLang glyph is not a static image; it is a **snapshot of a dynamic process**. A sequence of glyphs represents the evolution of this geometric object over time. This is where the true power of NGC becomes apparent.

*   **Residual Energy (||r||²):** Measures the degree of internal conflict or wasted cognitive energy. In a state of **Challenge** or unresolved tension, the residual energy will be high.

## Appendix A: Technical Specification

### A.1. Triaxial Coordinate Mapping for CoLang Glyphs

A CoLang glyph is represented as a geometric object `G` in NGC's triaxial polar coordinate space:

```
G = {(r_i, θ_i, φ_i) | i = 1, 2, ..., n}
```

Where:
*   `r_i` (radius): The magnitude or emphasis of the i-th thought-vector
*   `θ_i` (polar angle): The vertical orientation of the thought-vector
*   `φ_i` (azimuthal angle): The horizontal orientation of the thought-vector
*   `n`: The number of thought-vectors in the glyph

### A.2. Leibniz-Bocker Diagnostics for Glyph Sequences

Given a sequence of glyphs `{G_1, G_2, ..., G_m}`, we calculate the three diagnostics at each time step `t`:

**Coherence (ρ_t):**
```
ρ_t = (1/n) * Σ_i cos(angle(v_i, v_mean))
```
Where `v_i` is the i-th thought-vector and `v_mean` is the mean vector of all thought-vectors at time `t`.

**Curvature (Ω_t):**
```
Ω_t = ||dv_mean/dt||
```
The magnitude of the rate of change of the mean thought-vector.

**Residual Energy (||r||²_t):**
```
||r||²_t = Σ_i ||v_i - v_mean||²
```
The sum of squared deviations from the mean vector.

### A.3. Phase Transition Detection

A phase transition in the narrative arc is detected when:

```
ρ_t > ρ_threshold  AND  Ω_t < Ω_threshold  (after a period of high Ω)
```

This signals the transition from **Challenge** to **Resolution**.
